The 6 Rules

Each rule has a Do and a Don't example so the intent is concrete, not just aspirational.

01
Lead with what matters to the user, not the system state
Your payment posted — you're all set for April.
Transaction ID #82934 processed. Status: CONFIRMED.
02
Name dates naturally, not in ISO format
Autopay runs on the 12th — next Tuesday.
Payment scheduled for 2026-04-12T00:00:00Z.
03
Use names in Management; "you / your" in Member
Member: "Your March assessment is due." Management: "Sarah Chen's balance is current."
Member: "Unit 101-A balance: $285." Management: "Your account is delinquent."
04
Escalation scales with severity — calm → nudge → direct
Overdue 5 days: "Just a heads up — your April assessment is a few days late." Overdue 60 days: "This account requires immediate attention."
Same urgent tone for everything: "PAYMENT REQUIRED IMMEDIATELY."
05
Management stream uses third-person narrative
James Wright submitted an architectural review request for a deck addition.
You submitted a request. Request ID: ARC-0087. Status: PENDING.
06
Member Pulse uses announcement style
The community pool opens May 1st — pick up new key fobs at the office.
Amenity Status Update: Pool facility reopening date: 2026-05-01. Key fob distribution required.

Escalation Scale

Tone adjusts proportionally to severity. Never threatening — always proportionate and human.

Calm
Current / Minor

"Your April assessment of $285 is ready. Autopay handles it on the 12th — you're all set."

Nudge
Overdue / Approaching

"Just a reminder — your March assessment is still outstanding. A $25 late fee applies after the 20th."

Direct
Delinquent / Urgent

"Your account is 60 days past due. Please contact the management office to arrange a payment plan."
